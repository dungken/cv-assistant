# Finance-Specific CV Writing Tips

To stand out in the competitive finance industry, your CV must be data-driven, concise, and focused on quantifiable results.

## 1. Quantify Everything

Finance is a world of numbers. Don't just list responsibilities; show your impact.

- **Bad:** Managed a budget for the marketing department.
- **Good:** Optimized a $50k monthly marketing budget, reducing waste by 15% whilst maintaining lead volume.
- **Good:** Performed financial modeling for 5+ M&A transactions totaling $200M in value.

## 2. Highlight Technical Skills

Be specific about the tools and methodologies you use:

- **Financial Modeling:** DCF, LBO, Three-statement modeling.
- **Software:** Advanced Excel (VBA, Macros), Bloomberg Terminal, SAP, Oracle Financials, SQL, Python for Data Analysis.
- **Reporting:** IFRS, GAAP, SEC filings.

## 3. Professional Certifications

Include your status for key certifications (CFA, CPA, ACCA, etc.). If you are a candidate, specify the level:

- "CFA Level II Candidate"
- "Passed CPA Exam (4/4 parts)"

## 4. Structure & Format

- **Length:** Keep it to 1-2 pages maximum.
- **Layout:** Use reverse chronological order.
- **Keywords:** Incorporate keywords from the job description like "Risk Management", "Quantitative Analysis", or "Capital Markets".

## 5. Strong Personal Statement

Start with a 3-4 sentence summary of your experience, key achievements, and career goals specifically in the finance sector.
